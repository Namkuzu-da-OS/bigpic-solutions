# Crypto & Alternative Assets — Week Ending 2026-02-20

## Summary

The crypto bear market deepened this week as Bitcoin tested the lower bound of its consolidation range, trading between $65,600 and $71,000 before closing near $68,000 on February 20 — essentially flat week-over-week. The broader narrative shifted decisively bearish: Bloomberg published a front-page piece framing Bitcoin's "$1 trillion identity crisis," Fortune declared this the worst start to a year for BTC and ETH in a decade, and K33 Research compared current price action to the late 2022 bear market bottom. Google searches for "Bitcoin to zero" hit a record in the U.S. on February 22. Spot volumes fell 59% week-over-week, perpetual futures open interest slid to a four-month low, and funding rates remained negative across the board — classic capitulation indicators.

The mining sector absorbed a seismic event: Bitcoin difficulty spiked 14.73% on February 19 to 144.4 trillion — the largest absolute increase in network history and the biggest percentage jump since China's 2021 mining ban — as hash rate rebounded from approximately 884 EH/s to 1,030 EH/s following a US winter storm that had knocked an estimated 200 EH/s offline. Hash price remains at multi-year lows ($23.9/PH/day), squeezing all but the most efficient operators.

Bitcoin spot ETFs posted net outflows for the week: -$133.3M on February 18, -$165.8M on February 19, partially offset by +$88.1M on February 20 (the post-Presidents' Day session). Ethereum ETFs continued their outflow trend, with -$41.8M on February 18 alone. Nearly $4 billion has left Bitcoin ETFs over the past five weeks — a record peak-to-trough decline of 103,113 BTC since early October. Stablecoin market cap held steady at $314 billion, indicating capital remains parked on the sideline rather than exiting the ecosystem.

The regulatory front saw significant movement: the Senate Agriculture Committee advanced a crypto market structure bill (granting CFTC authority over digital commodities) for the first time past committee on January 29, and White House talks on stablecoin yield provisions made "progress" between banks and crypto stakeholders during the week, with a March 1 compromise deadline. The GENIUS Act implementation timeline marches toward its July 18 regulatory deadline.

## Halving Cycle Positioning

Bitcoin is now approximately 10 months post-April 2024 halving. The traditional 4-year cycle would place 2026 as a bear/consolidation year, analogous to 2014 and 2018. Fidelity's Jurrien Timmer stated the October 2025 high of ~$125,000 after 145 weeks of rallying "fits pretty well with what one might expect" from the historical pattern, calling 2026 a likely "year off" [12]. Others argue the cycle is evolving into a liquidity-driven 5-year phase due to institutional participation via ETFs. The consensus forming: 2026 may be the softest post-halving bear market in Bitcoin history, with ETF flows providing a structural floor that prior cycles lacked.

## Mining

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Hash Rate | ~1,030 EH/s (recovered from 884 EH/s post-storm) |
| Difficulty | 144.4T (+14.73% on Feb 19 — record absolute increase) |
| Hash Price | $23.9/PH/day (multi-year low) |
| Block Time | Normalizing after disruption |

The February 19 difficulty adjustment was historic: +14.73% to 144.4 trillion, an absolute gain of approximately 18.5 trillion — the largest single adjustment in Bitcoin's history in raw terms, and the biggest percentage jump since China's 2021 mining ban [16][17]. The spike reverses the prior -11% adjustment on February 9 that was triggered by the US winter storm knocking an estimated 200 EH/s of mining power offline. Hash rate has recovered from approximately 884 EH/s back above 1,000 EH/s (1 ZH/s), though still below the January all-time high [18].

**Miner Profitability:** Hash price remains at multi-year lows around $23.9/PH/day, down from the record low of $33.31 earlier in February [19]. The combination of depressed BTC prices (~50% below the October 2025 ATH of ~$126,000) and elevated difficulty is creating severe margin compression. Only the most efficient operators with access to cheap power (sub-$0.04/kWh) are generating positive margins. This environment accelerates consolidation and the ongoing pivot by publicly traded miners (MARA, RIOT, CLSK) toward HPC/AI hosting diversification.

## Outlook

The crypto market is exhibiting classic late-stage capitulation characteristics: extreme fear sentiment (Fear & Greed at 8), collapsed spot volumes (-59% WoW), negative funding rates across perpetual futures, and maximum pessimism in mainstream media. Historically, these conditions have preceded bottoming processes, though timing remains uncertain.

**Bull case:** $314B in stablecoins represents massive dry powder. Whale accumulation of 53,000 BTC over two weeks signals smart money positioning. Two major regulatory catalysts (GENIUS Act implementation, market structure bill) in Q2-Q3 could reignite institutional flows. The halving cycle's structural supply reduction continues compressing available BTC.

**Bear case:** ETF outflows have reached $3.8B over five weeks with no reversal signal. The ETH/BTC ratio collapse (-32.7% in one month) suggests risk appetite is deteriorating, not stabilizing. Hash rate recovery driving difficulty to record levels will squeeze miners further, potentially forcing liquidations. The traditional 4-year cycle places 2026 squarely in bear territory.

**Base case:** Range-bound consolidation between $60,000-$75,000 through Q1, with regulatory catalysts in Q2 determining the next directional move. The structural floor provided by ETF infrastructure and corporate treasury demand makes this cycle's bear market shallower than prior ones, but the ceiling is capped by macro headwinds and institutional de-risking.
